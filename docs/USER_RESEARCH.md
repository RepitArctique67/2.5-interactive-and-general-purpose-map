# User Research

## Personas

### 1. The History Enthusiast (Hobbyist)

- **Name**: Arthur, 45
- **Background**: High school history teacher, loves maps and timelines.
- **Goals**: Visualize historical events in context, show students how borders changed.
- **Frustrations**: Static maps in textbooks, hard to correlate events with geography.
- **Tech Savvy**: Moderate.

### 2. The Data Analyst (Professional)

- **Name**: Sarah, 29
- **Background**: GIS Specialist at an NGO.
- **Goals**: Overlay custom datasets on a global map, analyze trends over time.
- **Frustrations**: Existing tools are too expensive (ArcGIS) or too simple (Google Earth).
- **Tech Savvy**: High.

### 3. The Curious Explorer (Casual)

- **Name**: Leo, 22
- **Background**: Student, enjoys browsing Wikipedia and Reddit.
- **Goals**: Explore interesting facts, "doomscroll" through history, find cool visualizations.
- **Frustrations**: Clunky interfaces, slow loading times.
- **Tech Savvy**: High (mobile-first).

## Interview Guide

### Objectives

- Understand how users currently visualize historical/geospatial data.
- Identify pain points in existing tools.
- Validate the need for a "time-traveling" map.

### Questions

1. **Intro**: Tell me about the last time you looked for a map to understand a historical event.
2. **Tools**: What tools did you use? (Google Maps, Wikipedia, physical atlas?)
3. **Pain Points**: What was frustrating about that experience?
4. **Feature Validation**: If you could have a slider to change the year on Google Maps, what would you use it for?
5. **Data**: What kind of data would you be most interested in seeing overlaid on the globe? (Climate, Population, Borders, Battles?)

## Feedback Collection Strategy

### Channels

- **In-App Feedback Widget**: Simple "Report Issue" or "Suggest Feature" button in the Tools panel.
- **Community Discord/Slack**: Direct engagement with power users.
- **Usage Analytics**: Tracking most used layers and average session time.

### Synthesis

- Weekly review of feedback tickets.
- Tagging requests by category (Data, UI, Performance).
- Updating the [Roadmap](./ROADMAP.md) based on user demand.
